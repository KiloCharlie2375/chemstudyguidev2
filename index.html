Fundamentals

Core definitions: Nomenclature, polyatomic ions, bonding types, Lewis structures, oxidation states.

Calculations

Essential calculation tools: molar mass, unit conversions, concentration units, stoichiometry, gas laws.

Find molar mass by summing atomic masses from the periodic table. Convert mass ↔ moles ↔ particles ↔ volume (gases).

  • Mass to moles: n = mass / M
  • Moles to particles: N = n × 6.022×10⁲³
  • Moles to volume (STP): V = n × 22.4 L
  • Molarity (M): mol solute / L solution
  • Molality (m): mol solute / kg solvent
  • Percent by mass: (mass solute / mass solution) × 100
  • Percent by volume: (volume solute / volume solution) × 100
  • Mole fraction: X = mol component / total mol
  • Dilution: M₁V₁ = M₂V₂

Use balanced equations and mole ratios to relate reactants and products. Key steps:

  1. Balance the equation.
  2. Convert given mass or volume to moles.
  3. Apply mole ratio.
  4. Convert moles back to required units.
  • Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT (R = 0.0821 L·atm/mol·K)
  • Combined Gas Law: P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂
  • Partial pressures: Dalton’s Law, P_total = ΣP_i
  • Effusion: Graham’s Law, rate ∝ 1/√M
